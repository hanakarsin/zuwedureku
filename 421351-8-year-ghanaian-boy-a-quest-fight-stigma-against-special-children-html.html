8-Year-Old Boy on Quest to Fight Stigma against Special Needs Children

March 02, 2024
Post
  • A protective elder brother by the name of Jojo is determined to fight the stigma against his special needs sister Ayeyie
  • In an interview, Jojo explained the condition of his younger sister to be cerebral palsy which affects her movement and coordination
  • Parents of Ayeyie revealed that they have faced stigmatisation from both church members and families

An 8-year-old Ghanaian boy with a special needs sister, Ayeyie, is on a quest to stop the stigma against people with disabilities.

In a documentary on the Facebook page of TV3 News, the young man by the name of Jojo Oti Yeboah described his sister's condition.

Jojo said his sister suffers from cerebral palsy (CP), which he described as a congenital disorder that affects movement and coordination.

Stigmatised

The protective brother revealed that Ayeyie caught jaundice on the third day of birth which affected the brain and prevents her from controlling her movements.

"My sister can't do anything by herself and it makes me feel sad," said Jojo.

Ayeyie's mother revealed that she had to stop going to church because of the stigma she faced over there.

Ayeyie's father disclosed that he was asked by his friends to run away from the family because of his daughter's condition but he ignored them.

Jojo's Desire

Young Jojo's desire is to be given a platform to educate the public to accept children living with disabilities.

He also added that his biggest wish for the sister is to see her walk someday.

His final words to everybody was that we should learn to love people with disabilities.

Kenya's CP Champion

For an eight-year-old, the realisation that children with special needs need love, acceptance and understanding puts him miles ahead of many adults who are yet to understand the condition.

In an exclusive interview with Kenya's Rose Angira whose son Elly suffers from the same condition, she lamented the public's misguided notion that such disability is a curse or punishment for the parents' sins.

It is the reason she was inspired to become a CP champion in a bid to create awareness and help the public understand it better.
