Youths ask Uhuru to waive HELB loan, clear them from CRB blacklist to support BBI

March 20, 2024
Post

- President Uhuru Kenyatta offered Members of County Assembly (MCAs) a KSh 2 million car grant

- The move was interpreted by the anti-BBI team as a bribe for the ward representatives to pass the BBI Bill

- A section of youths have, in turn, come up with their own irreducible minimums for them to support the BBI

A move by President Uhuru Kenyatta to give Members of County Assembly (MCAs) a KSh 2 million car grant has continued to elicit mixed reactions with the majority claiming it was a 'bribe' for them to back the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

A section of youths have, in turn, come up with their own irreducible minimums to the BBI proponents led by Uhuru for them to support the report.

Using the hashtag #CRBandHELB, the youths implored the head of state to waive their education loan and clear them from Credit Reference Bureau (CRB) for them back the BBI report.

Reactions

TUKO.co.ke sampled some of the reactions from the tag which trended top on Friday, February 5.

Wilson Mathu said: "In the car grant spirit, our CRB and HELB loans must be cleared before we pass this thing."Bluepostboy opined: "If MCA is given KSh 2000000 to pass BBI then fresh Graduates should be CRB and HELB loan clearance to pass the same, we need a robust conversation because this country not only belong to politicians."

Not a bribe

Former prime minister Raila Odinga said he supports the proposal to give ward representatives across the country car grants as directed by Uhuru during his meeting with MCAs from Mt Kenya on Friday, January 29.

The Opposition leader maintained the car grant was not a bribe for them to pass the BBI Bill but a right that the county lawmakers were entitled to get just like MPs.

"Some people are saying the car grant is a bribe so that you (MCAs) can pass the BBI, that is not true, we give car grants to MPs, why are they not saying it is a bribe? I understand the challenges you face and you deserve a car grant just like MPs do," he said.

Siaya county leads

As previously reported by TUKO.co.ke, Siaya county became the first of all the 47 devolved units to pass the BBI report.

The assembly approved the document within 24 hours after receiving it from the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC).

More counties are expected to debate and make a decision on the Bill starting next week when they resume sittings after a long recess.
