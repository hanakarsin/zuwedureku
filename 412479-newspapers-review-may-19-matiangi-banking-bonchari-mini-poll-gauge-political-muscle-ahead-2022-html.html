Newspapers Review for May 19: Matiang'i Was Banking on Bonchari Mini-Poll to Gauge Muscle Ahead of 2

July 17, 2024
Post

On Wednesday, May 19, the newspapers widely reported on the Juja and Bonchari parliamentary by-elections as well as the Rurii Ward mini-poll which were all largely peaceful but were dotted with instances of drama.

The papers also reported about Deputy President William Ruto's allies's decision not to impeach President Uhuru Kenyatta after the BBI judgment found him in contravention of the law.

1. The Standard

According to the Standard, Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i has been using the Bonchari parliamentary by-election to test his influence and popularity in Gusii land as he gears up for 2022.

Though the super CS is yet to publicly announce his interest in the presidency, some of his colleagues in the Cabinet have been vouching for him.

It had been anticipated that Jubilee Party would not field a candidate, but Matiang'i made a last-minute decision to field ex-MP Zebedeo Opore to be on the ballot on a Jubilee ticket.

Matiang'i's handlers revealed the CS, who is seen as President Uhuru Kenyatta's point man in Gusii land, was using the by-election to measure his political muscles.

However, the ODM candidate Pavel Oimeke emerged as the winner of the hotly contested mini poll with 8,049 votes, while Opore came second with 7, 279 votes.

2. Daily Nation

According to this paper, Deputy President William Ruto's allies have dismissed claims they will use the Thursday, May 13, BBI judgment as grounds to impeach President Uhuru Kenyatta.

This is after lawyer Charles Njama said any MP who had the interest to oust the president could do so as the High Court had ruled that Uhuru had contravened the law while initiating the Building Bridges Initiative.

Led by Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagwa, the Tanga Tanga faction said its eyes were on the 2022 General Election and would let the president complete his final term in peace.

Belgut MP Nelson Koech echoed similar sentiments and said impeaching the head of state has never crossed the minds of any of his counterparts.

3. Taifa Leo

According to Taifa Leo, it has emerged that President Uhuru Kenyatta saved Bungoma governor Wycliffe Wangamati from impeachment.

This is after 45 Bungoma Members of County Assembly (MCAs) endorsed an impeachment motion against the governor on the grounds of abuse of office and misappropriation of COVID-19 funds.

Minority leader Francis Chemion was asked to drop the motion during a meeting as orders from Jubilee had advised so.

The MCAs said they had to follow the orders which came from Uhuru himself, and they later met with the governor to iron out their issues.

4. The Star

Several Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) soldiers are feared dead after their patrol vehicle ran over an improvised explosive device at Baure in Lamu East.

The IED is suspected to have been planted by al Shabaab militants hiding in Boni Forest, and it exploded on the morning of Tuesday, May 18.

Lamu county commissioner Irungu Macharia said the exact number of casualties was unknown as the area has poor communication signal and tough terrain.

5. People Daily

According to this publication, the National Assembly will today, May 19, hold a special meeting to reject or approve the nomination of chief justice designate, Martha Koome.

In a gazette notice, Speaker Justin Muturi asked members to avail themselves for the special session.

Majority Leader Amos Kimunya confirmed the House would convene to decide on Koome's fate who, if approved, will become Kenya's first female chief justice.

On Thursday, May 13, the Justice and Legal Affairs Committee vetted the lady justice and retreated to a Nairobi hotel on Tuesday, May 18, to make its final report.
