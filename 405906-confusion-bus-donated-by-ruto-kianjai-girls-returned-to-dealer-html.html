Confusion as School Bus Donated by Ruto to Kianjai Girls' Returned to Dealer

Tigania West residents have been left confused after a bus that Deputy President William Ruto donated to Kianjai Girls’ Secondary School during his tour in Meru county was taken back to the the company that assembled it.

The vehicle valued at KSh 3.5 million was reportedly taken back to Nairobi.

According to Nation, the area residents condemned the move claiming the school bus handover ceremony held on Saturday, March 6, was meant to hoodwink them.

They further claimed that the school bus was to be taken to another school.

Yet to be completed

Tigania West MP John Mutunga, in his defence, said the vehicle was taken back by the assembling company so that it could be completed.

The lawmaker claimed the company only released the vehicle for the official handover ceremony even though it was yet to be completed.

"The bus was yet to be completely assembled due to the emergence of COVID-19. The company allowed us to use it for Ruto's official function then return it so that it could be completed," said Mutunga.

Another group in the area identified as Tigania Eye claimed the vehicle, which was fully funded by the constituency's development fund was repossessed as it was yet to be paid for fully.

A day later, the Isuzu East Africa company, which assembled the vehicle admitted to having taken it back for body fabrication.

The company said the bus has since been completed, number plate issued and will be delivered to the school.
