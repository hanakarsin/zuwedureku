Uhuru, Raila to meet MPs and MCAs to chart way forward for BBI

The journey of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) bill to the National Assembly has begun in earnest.

This was after political party leaders led by President Uhuru Kenyatta and the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) agreed to hold talks with MPs and county assemblies leaderships.

The two leaders alongside ANC boss Musalia Mudavadi and Ford Kenya's Moses Wetang'ula held a round table discussion at the State House on Thursday, February 25.

Also present were Wiper Democratic Movement party leader Kalonzo Musyoka, KANU chairperson and Senator Gideon Moi as well as NARC's Charity Ngilu.

A statement from the State House sent to the newsrooms later after the meeting confirmed the leaders identified need for civic education on the content of the BBI bill.

It was for this reason that a consultative meeting will be convened on March 9, to chart the way forward for the BBI ahead of its introduction in the parliament.

"And to this end we shall hold a joint consultative meeting on the March 9, 2021, involving Members of Parliament and counties leadership. As our county representatives have shown, now is the moment for all Kenyans to put aside partisan divides and come together to build a better Kenya," they said in a joint statement.

Road to referendum

Uhuru and the Opposition chief are expected to launch the nationwide BBI campaign on Monday, March 1. after being adopted by more than 24 counties.

Political heavyweight including regional kingpins and select governors allied to the president and the ODM leaders, clerics, trade unionists and civil society leaders will be tasked with popularising the report.

Overwhelming support

Baringo and Nandi counties became the first and the second respectively to reject the proposed legislation.

As on Thursday, 41 counties had adopted the bill surpassing the magic 24 counties which is required for such a legislation to move forward.

This therefore means Baringo and Nandi's rejection of the draft will have little to no effect on the progress of the BBI as the document achieved the constitutionally required threshold.
