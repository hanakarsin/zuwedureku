Water supply in the Nairobi Central Business District (CBD) has been disrupted after the expressway contractor erroneously destroyed water pipes.

The Nairobi Water and Sewerage Company, in a statement on Wednesday, April 21, said it had cut off the supply to allow for repairs of the pipes.

The company urged Kenya's capital dwellers to be patient as efforts to restore supply began.

“Water supply to the entire CBD has been shut down due to pipe damage by the Expressway Contractor. The line has been shut to pave way for repairs,” read the statement.

Toll road

As previously reported, Transport Cabinet Secretary James Macharia officially gazetted the Nairobi Expressway as a toll road.

The gazettement meant motorists would pay a fee to use the road, which will be completed in 2022.

According to the gazette notice made on Thursday, December 31, the Expressway that was declared a toll road would start from African Inland Church, Mlolongo, all the way to James Gichuru Road.

“In exercise of the powers conferred by section 3 (2A) of the Public Roads Toll Act, (Cap 407), the dual carriageway with Class A standard that connects the Mlolongo with James Gichuru road along the median strip of the A8 National Road are declared to be Toll Roads with immediate effect,” the notice read in part.

However, the communique did not specify the amount that motorists will pay, but reports indicate they will vary from KSh 100 to KSh 1,600 according to the type of motorists.

Similarly, ambulances, police and military vehicles will be exempted from paying to use the road.
