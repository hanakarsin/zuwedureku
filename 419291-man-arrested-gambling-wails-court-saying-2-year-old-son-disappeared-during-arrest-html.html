Man Arrested for Gambling Wails in Court Saying 2-Year-Old Son Disappeared During Arrest

April 22, 2024
Post
  • Anthony Baraza Okumu was arrested alongside other suspects for gambling in public
  • During the hearing of his case, Okumu cried, accusing police officers of taking away his son during his arrest
  • Okumu was also charged for lying to police officers about his real name when he was being arrested

A man arrested for gambling on Friday, July 16, brought things to a standstill at Milimani law court after wailing, claiming police officers had taken away his two-year-old son during his arrest.

Anthony Baraza Okumu started screaming immediately after taking a plea for gambling and lying about his real name.

"Moto wangu,mtoto wangu,nataka mtoto wangu, (my child, my child, I want my child)," he shouted as tears rolled down his cheeks.

Son taken during his arrest

Okumu maintained that his son got lost when the police bundled him alongside other suspects into the police lorry at Machakos country bus station.

According to the charge sheet, the suspect is said to have lied about his name to a police officer.

He was also charged for gambling in a public place contrary to the betting, loitering and gambling Act.

Pleaded not guilty

Appearing before Senior Principal Magistrate Caroline Muthoni, Okumu pleaded not guilty and was freed on a cash bail of KSh 50,000.

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), on May 11, warned members of the public of a syndicate operating within the city.

The DCI said the gamblers targets crowded areas such as bus stages where there are many gullible passers-by.

This comes a month after the Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB) banned radio stations from running lotteries and prize competitions over rampant fraud.

Mbugi reiterated that radio stations' involvement in gaming activities had created a perfect ground for illegal activities.
