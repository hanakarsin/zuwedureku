Despite the fact that Africans have unique cultures, the advent of colonialism brought change in the name of civilization; a change that drove away a crucial element of the African social set-up. This saw some Africans adopt the ways of the foreigner at the expense of her own culture. One of the most notable changes is adoption of Christianity. Christianity came with a slight change in the naming process of a child. Unlike the past, children born of Christian African parents are either given a Christian first name or an English name, and a traditional second name -- a clan name of a paternal or maternal relative.
