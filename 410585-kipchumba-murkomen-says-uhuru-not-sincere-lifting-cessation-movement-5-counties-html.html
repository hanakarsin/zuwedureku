Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen has criticised President Uhuru Kenyatta for lifting the cessation of movement in Nairobi, Machakos, Nakuru, Kiambu, and Kajiado counties on Saturday, May 1.

In a short statement issued on his Twitter page, the former Senate majority leader said the reopening of the five counties was informed by Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) politics and not pleas by Kenyans.

"The lifting of cessation of movement and relaxation of COVID-19 rules is sponsored by BBI referendum and not your petitions," Murkomen tweeted.

Uhuru relaxes COVID-19 measures

While lifting the cessation of movement in the mentioned counties, the president said empirical evidence showed a reduction in coronavirus positivity rate.

"With regard to the zoned area comprising of the counties of Nairobi, Machakos, Kiambu, Kajiado, and Nakuru, it is directed that the cessation of movement into and out of the Zoned area be and is hereby lifted," he stated during the Labour Day celebrations.

At the same time, Uhuru revised curfew hours in the zoned area to commence at 10:00 pm and end at 4:00 am from 8 pm with effect from mid-night May 1.

He also allowed bars and restaurants to reopen again as the rate of new coronavirus infections slowly declines across the country.

Kenyans on social media praised the head of state after he revised COVID-19 containment measures that gave them a sigh of relief.

The commander-in-chief of the armed forces had on March 26 said there would be no movement into and out of Nakuru, Kiambu, Nairobi, Kajiado, and Machakos counties over the surge in COVID-19 infections at the time.

"That there shall be a cessation of all movement by road, rail, or air into and out of the disease infected area as one zoned area comprising of the counties of Nairobi, Kajiado, Machakos, Kiambu, and Nakuru, effective Saturday, March 27, 2021; until otherwise notified," he directed.
