Meru senator Mithika Linturi terms estranged wife as slay queen in court

May 10, 2024
Post

- Linturi says his estranged wife Marianne Kitany was a version of slay queen

- Linturi said denying him access to his Runda house would set precedence for unmarried women to claim ownership of matrimonial property

- He reiterated he was in a monogamous marriage with wife Mary Kaimenyi for 18 years

- Kitany insisted she was married to Linturi through customary laws

Meru senator Mithika Linturi has gone to court seeking lifting of orders barring him from accessing their co-owned home with estranged wife Marianne Kitany saying she has never been his wife.

In a thin-veiled jibe to Kitany through his lawyer Muthomi Thiankolu, Linturi referred to the estranged wife as a slay queen who had no authority to seek orders barring him access to his home.

Speaking during the hearing of the application in court, Muthomi called on the court to dismiss initial prayers granted to Kitany as she was illegible to obtain such orders.

"If slay queens and prostitutes can rush to court and obtain orders as if those lawfully married don’t exist, the court must remove itself from making an adversarial position,” he argued on Tuesday, January 15.

According to Muthomi, Kitany obtained the said orders after claiming she was in a domestic relationship with Linturi a statement he termed as untrue.

The lawyer instead argued that Linturi was in a monogamous union for 18 years with wife Mary Kaimenyi.

Linturi said that by the court upholding orders barring him from accessing his Runda home, it would mean all marriages in the country were polygamous.

He said Kitany had gone to desperate lengths to show he had capacity to enter a customary marriage with her through using fake documents.

“The documents are fake because Linturi has never filed a divorce matter between him and his wife Mary Kaimenyi. There is a letter from Catholic church priest in Tigania that solemnise the marriage,” stated Linturi’s lawyer.

According to the senator, the orders if extended, will be tantamount to encouraging adultery and diminishing the institution of marriage.

The court further heard there was no evidence to show Linturi had caused domestic violence and Kitany was not one of those marginalised or vulnerable women as she claimed because she owns property in Kileleshwa, South B and Lavington estates.

According to the senator, he has never established any domestic or marital relationship with Kitany or any of her children and they were merely guests at his Runda House.

Kitany’s lawyer Danstan Omari asked the court to extend the said orders in a bid to restore the dignity of women arguing it would be a great calamity for men to use women and dump them.

Omari told the court that Kitany’s personal clothing were found in the Runda house saying they had spent quality time together since 2005.

“My client has an inherent dignity which should be protected as violence demeans a person in that relationship. It’s our submission that Kitany has faced the gravest of injustices occasioned by a person so loving, dear, caring only to turn out to be the greatest tormentor,” said Omari.

According to Kitany’s lawyer, the court has jurisdiction to grant orders it issued whether a person is referred to as a prostitute, slay queen or any word that is used in a stay and dump relationship.

Senior Resident Magistrate Isaac Orenge will rule on whether eviction orders granted against Linturi would be issued permanently on Friday, February 1.
