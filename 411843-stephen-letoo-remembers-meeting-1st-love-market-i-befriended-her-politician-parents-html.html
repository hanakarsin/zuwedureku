The father of one, who comes from the Maasai community, has narrated exclusively to TUKO.co.ke about how he met his first love. They were in Form Two at that time.

"I admired her when I met her the first day in Enoosaen market in Trans Mara, Narok county. My appetite for her escalated when she clapped for me when I narrated a biblical poem in my local church," Letoo recalled.

The Citizen TV reporter said that he used her brother to get to her and became so close to her parents.

"I befriended her parents, who were politicians so that I could be in their home at any point without being seen as a visitor," Letoo narrated.

The two dated for five years and while in their third year in university, she relocated to the US. Despite being married, Letoo is planning to visit her.

"She was a good person and I'm happy she made it in life. She has a beautiful family and I am happy for her. I will be visiting her soon," he said.

On May 9, during Mother's Day, Letoo left fans awed after celebrating his mother and her co-wife.

He said polygamy is better than monogamy as he wished his two mothers a happy Mother's Day.

Letoo narrated how he was raised and treated equally by his two mothers, noting that a polygamous family setup provides a better arrangement for development and family growth.
