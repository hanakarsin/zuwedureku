Jalang'o: Mixed Reactions from Kenyans after Comedian Distanced Himself from Wash-Wash Activities

June 18, 2024
Post
  • Comedian Jalang'os decision to deny that he is an accomplice to a money-laundering syndicate seems to have come back to bite him
  • This is because a cross-section of the public have questioned why it is only him out of those implicated who chose to speak out
  • Others faulted him for always displaying his wealth, yet people who have clean money are known to be humble about it

Radio presenter and upcoming politician Jalang'o started the week by struggling to clear his name over allegations that his wealth is ill-gotten.

According to a recent exposé, the philanthropist is part of a syndicate that deals in printing fake currency and money laundering.

He maintained that he hustles for his money, from the radio job, to brand ambassador gigs, to running an advertising agency.

Some agreed with him

Being a public figure, a good number of people sided with him, comparing him to the proverbial fruit tree that always gets pelted with stones.

Among those who defended the entrepreneur was detective Jane Mugoh who posited that Jalang'os name is the subject of mudslinging by people who are jealous of his achievements.

"I have been a victim of false accusations, it pains me. Jalas is an intelligent, hardworking and generous man I have personally worked with. Jealousy will never win," said the detective.

According to this school of thought, the entertainer is targeted because of his recent announcement that he will be vying for a parliamentary seat.

Olilo Jnr:

"The moment you show interest in a political seat, you must be ready to face anything."

Dorothy Santos:

"Jalang'o has built himself into a brand. I doubt he would so foolish as to destroy it by doing dubious deals."

Juma Boy:

"Only lazy and envious individuals have the time to throw all lame accusations on one's wealth even though they work smart and hard to have what they have."

No need to defend himself

A cross-section argued that it was not necessary for the self-made millionaire to defend himself that fervently if he knows the allegations are false.

Karis Olen Peter:

"He did wrong to defend himself. Be a man at times by keeping quiet and letting them discuss you until they get tired and sleep."

Ted Dy:

"His reaction makes him look guilty enough. Even his physical appearance betrays him, he always appears dishonest to me."

Jennypha Mamake Hawaii:

"We have not seen any of the mentioned people defending themselves the way Jalas did and they hang out together, which makes the suspicion correct."

Dudu Mbaya:

"But he hangs around the people who have been mentioned in the biggest scams then talks about integrity. He should have just kept quiet like the big bosses and let it wash over."

Faulted over flamboyant lifestyle

Others took a stab at him for living a flamboyant lifestyle, adding that those with the real wealth are subtle about it.

Naomi Adieri:

"People like Churchill and Otoyo are very silent with their wealth but when you want to show off your houses, cars and land on social media, expect a lot of rumours."

Anne Wanjiku:

"Keep your wealth a secret because you are putting words in people's mouths and not everyone is happy for your progress. My brother, be cool."

Peter Ndegwa:

"Rumours are bound to circulate as long as you advertise your wealth on social media."

Up until that point, one is left to wonder whether it was wrong for the comedian to defend himself against the allegations.
