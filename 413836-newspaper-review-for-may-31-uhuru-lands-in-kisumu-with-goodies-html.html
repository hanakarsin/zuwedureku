Newspapers Review For May 31: Uhuru Lands In Kisumu With Goodies

February 02, 2024
Post

It is Monday, May 31. Today's headlines have revolved around President Uhuru Kenyatta's three-day working tour in the Nyanza region.

The dailies also spared space for the former Kibwezi Member of Parliament, Kalembe Ndile, who died on Sunday, May 30, after battling a long illness.

1. People Daily

The daily reported on the warm welcome accorded to Uhuru after he landed in Kisumu to kick off his tour.

He will be leading the country in marking the 58th Madaraka Day ceremony.

Soon after landing at the Kisumu International Airpot, the president boarded his airforce chopper headed to the neighbouring Siaya county, where he commissioned the water and sanitation project in Ndanu, Yala.

The KSh 2.4 billion project was funded by the national government in collaboration with the African Development Bank.

The project will serve more than 200,000 people in five centres at Nyangweso, Wagai, Ng'iya, Nyagondo, and Kogelo.

The president also officially opened the Kodiaga-Wagai-Onyinyore and Nyangweso-Mukanda roads.

2. Daily Nation

The publication also focused on the president's Nyanza tour, which began on Sunday, May 30.

Daily Nation highlighted the busy day Uhuru will be having on Monday, May 31, as he launches a number of projects in Kisumu.

In the company of ODM chief Raila Odinga and other region's leaders, the president is expected to launch the revamped Kisumu port, which is said to have cost KSh 3 billion.

The head of state will also be flagging off the refurbished MV Uhuru vessel, making its way to Jinja, Uganda.

The vessel will be loaded with 22 wagons, each weighing 50,000 litres of petroleum products.

The renovation works at the port has been ongoing for the past two years.

Uhuru will also launch the new shipyard managed by the Kenya Navy, the KSh 476 million maritime school, the Simu Inland Container Depot and Mamboleo's Jomo Kenyatta International Stadium, which will be hosting the Madaraka Day fete on Tuesday, June 1.

3. The Standard

The daily was also not left behind in spotlighting the president's tour in a part of the larger Nyanza region.

During the visit, Uhuru broke his silence on the setback that hit the Constitution Amendment Bill 2020, which was nullified by a five-judge bench.

Speaking in Siaya, the president was confident in the presumption of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) reggae after the Court of Appeal hopefully quashes the ruling made on May 13 by the High Court judges.

Uhuru placed his bet on such lawyers as James Orengo, whom he said were capable of overturning the ruling.

After Raila had joined Uhuru in Siaya, his chopper crashed at Kudho Primary School in Gem, Siaya County, where the ODM chief had been dropped.

According to a police report, the helicopter, Bell 407, registration 5Y-PSM had tried to take off before crashing.

A statement from Raila's spokesperson, Dennis Onyango indicated that the former prime minister was safe.

Raila proceeded with his scheduled activities with the president in the county.

4. Taifa Leo

President Uhuru Kenyatta's tour in Kisumu is still the main deal in the Swahili daily.

Out of the 2018 handshake between the president and Raila Odinga, the Luo-Nyanza region will be reaping big out of Uhuru's projects as outlined in the blueprint.

On day one of the working visit, the president already commissioned a water project and opened some roads in Siaya.

Other projects will be launched on Monday, May 31, as Taifa Leo reports.

The major projects slated for opening in Kisumu are the Uhuru Business Complex built at the cost of KSh 600 million and the KSh 16 billion 310-kilometre Olkaria Lessos-Kisumu high voltage transmission line at the Kibos sub-station.

Nonetheless, the president with his handshake partner missed the long-awaited maiden train ride from Kisumu to Nakuru and back.

The trip could not happen as scheduled since 23.5km of the line is still under construction.

Also incomplete are the Mamboleo-Kisumu-Chemelil, the Masara-Miru, the Sori-Mbita, and the Siaya-Usenge roads.

5. The Star

Away from the Nyanza tour, Kalembe Ndile's demise also caught attention.

The former lawmaker died on Sunday, May 30, at the Nairobi Hospital, where he had been undergoing treatment for an undisclosed ailment.

The Star recalled the last message the late Kalembe had on the Machakos-based journalists during his last public appearance on May 23.

Kalembe hailed the Machakos media team for playing key roles in his life.

"Thanks for your support. You Machakos media team played key roles in my life. You visited me while I was sick. Remain focused and united,” he told one of the journalists in a phone call.

In their messages of condolence, President Uhuru Kenyatta described Kalembe as a determined leader who worked hard to acquire his status.

Also to mourn the fallen politician were ODM leader Raila Odinga, Deputy President William Ruto and Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka.

They eulogised Kalembe as a leader who valued equal justice and would spiritedly fight for the rights of the underprivileged Kenyans.

The politician had served as Kibwezi MP between 2002 and 2007. He had also served in different government parastatals.

He died aged 57 years.
