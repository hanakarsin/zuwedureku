Bifwoli Wakoli appointed to replace late Soita Shitanda

July 23, 2024
Post

- The government has handed state jobs to a number of familiar faces in the country

- Among them is former Bumula MP Bifwoli Wakoli who has been appinted to replace the late Soita Shitanda

- Nzamba Kitonga, John Mututho and former Changamwe MP Ramadhan Seif Kajembe have also been appointed

- A week ago, former Interior CS Joseph Ole Lenku and Baringo Senator Gideon Moi's wife, Zahra, were also handed state jobs

President Uhuru Kenyatta has given return route into the government a number of prominent faces including former Bumula MP and assistant minister Bifwoli Wakoli and head of the committee of experts that drafted the 2010 constitution Nzamba Kitonga.

Wakoli has been appointed the non-executive chairperson of the Agricultural Development Corporation Board  for a period of  three years with effect June 24th 2016.

His appointment is a replacement of former Malava MP Soita Shitanda who passed away in May, 2016.

Wakoli’s appointment gives him a return to the government once more having served as assistant minister in the ministry of lands in the grand coalition government.

He was elected Bumula MP in the 2007 election but failed to defend his seat in the 2013 general election.

Nzamba Kitonga has also been appointed the non-executive chairperson of the Council of Legal Education for a period of three years.

Kitonga is largely lauded for leading a team that led to the acquisition of the 2010 constitution which was largely accepted by Kenyans and promulgated on August 27, 2010.

He is also one of those touted to replace the retired Chief Justice Willy Mutunga. However, his ethnicity is likely to be a stumbling block considering Mutunga also comes from the Akamba community where Nzamba hails from.

As reported early by TUKO.co.ke, former police boss David Kimaiyo has been sacked by President Uhuru Kenyatta and his position as Kenya Airports Authority Board chairman taken by former Chief of Defence Forces General Julius Karangi.

Others who landed state jobs include NACADA Chairman John Mututho who has been appointed the non-executive chairperson of the Transport Licensing Appeals Board, Noah Katana Ngala the non-executive Chairperson of the Kenya National Library Board and former Changamwe MP Ramadhan Seif Kajembe as the non-executive chairperson of the Kenya Ferry Services Board.

They join former Interior Cabinet Secretary Joseph Ole Lenku and Baringo Senator Gideon Moi’s wife, Zahra, who were handed state jobs a week ago.

Ole Lenku was appointed chairman of the Sacco Societies Regulatory Authority. He was the first minister in Uhuru’s government to be sacked over incompetence.

Zahra Moi was appointed to the Tourism Research Institute board to replace Margaret Wawuda Mwakima whose early appointment was revoked.

Zahra has a Magna Cum Laude degree (First Class) in Bachelor of Science in International Business Administration from the United States International University (USIU).
